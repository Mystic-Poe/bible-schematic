# Daniel Chapter 12

**13 verses**

---

## Verse 1

> “At that time Michael shall stand up, The great prince who stands <i>watch</i> over the sons of your people;  And there shall be a time of trouble, Such as never was since there was a nation, <i>Even</i> to that time. And at that time your people shall be delivered, Every one who is found written in the book.

---

## Verse 2

> And many of those who sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake,  Some to everlasting life, Some to shame <i>and</i> everlasting contempt.

---

## Verse 3

> Those who are wise shall shine Like the brightness of the firmament,  And those who turn many to righteousness  Like the stars forever and ever.

---

## Verse 4

> “But you, Daniel, shut up the words, and seal the book until the time of the end; many shall run to and fro, and knowledge shall increase.”

---

## Verse 5

> Then I, Daniel, looked; and there stood two others, one on this riverbank and the other on that riverbank.

---

## Verse 6

> And <i>one</i> said to the man clothed in linen, who <i>was</i> above the waters of the river, “How long shall the fulfillment of these wonders <i>be?</i>”

---

## Verse 7

> Then I heard the man clothed in linen, who <i>was</i> above the waters of the river, when he held up his right hand and his left hand to heaven, and swore by Him who lives forever, that <i>it</i> <i>shall</i> <i>be</i> for a time, times, and half <i>a</i> <i>time;</i> and when the power of the holy people has been completely shattered, all these <i>things</i> shall be finished.

---

## Verse 8

> Although I heard, I did not understand. Then I said, “My lord, what <i>shall</i> <i>be</i> the end of these <i>things?</i>”

---

## Verse 9

> And he said, “Go <i>your</i> <i>way,</i> Daniel, for the words <i>are</i> closed up and sealed till the time of the end.

---

## Verse 10

> Many shall be purified, made white, and refined, but the wicked shall do wickedly; and none of the wicked shall understand, but the wise shall understand.

---

## Verse 11

> “And from the time <i>that</i> the daily <i>sacrifice</i> is taken away, and the abomination of desolation is set up, <i>there</i> <i>shall</i> <i>be</i> one thousand two hundred and ninety days.

---

## Verse 12

> Blessed <i>is</i> he who waits, and comes to the one thousand three hundred and thirty-five days.

---

## Verse 13

> “But you, go <i>your</i> <i>way</i> till the end; for you shall rest, and will arise to your inheritance at the end of the days.”

---
